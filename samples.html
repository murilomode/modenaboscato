01Payroll Close CycleHow month-end HR inputs moved from about two weeks to four days.Details
Before

Payroll-adjacent calculations and document checks were spread across manual routines, systems and follow-up messages.

Change

Used HR and benefit systems more consistently, cleaned inputs earlier, standardised the checks that remained manual and kept pending items visible.

Result

The monthly close window moved from roughly two weeks to four days without removing the compliance checks that still needed human review.

Metric basis

About 60% shorter when the previous two-week window is counted as 10 working days and compared with the new four-day close.

02Internal Communication WorkflowReplacing scattered requests with traceable communication.Details
Before

Important requests could sit inside email chains or informal messages, making ownership and follow-up harder to prove.

Change

Helped move institutional communication into 1Doc, using formal routing, visible ownership and documented responses.

Result

Missed internal requests were virtually eliminated because requests became traceable rather than dependent on memory or inbox timing.

Transfer

The same habit applies to HRIS case notes, manager enquiries, employee movements and payroll handoffs.

03Legacy Records CleanupHandling old employee data without trusting it blindly.Details
Context

The organisation has decades of records, which means old information can be incomplete, duplicated or simply wrong.

Risk

Bad HR data can affect payroll, benefits, attendance, travel payments, overtime records and employee confidence.

Control

Check the source document, confirm effective dates, update systems when new information reaches HR and keep the audit trail clear.

Volume

In a normal week, I touch more than 100 employee records or files through checks, updates, requests and follow-up.

05Benefits and Payroll Input ChecksMeal-benefit, travel and overtime data before it becomes a payroll issue.Details
Systems

Worked with Softnex Integra for food and meal benefit calculations, alongside HR systems and government platforms.

Common issue

If travel, overtime or leave is not communicated correctly, the employee can receive an incorrect deduction or miss an amount due.

Control

Confirm the record before deadline, check missing information, then update or route the payroll-relevant input.

Result

Faster calculation routines and fewer avoidable corrections during monthly closing.

06Department HR Entry PointCreating a clearer front door for HR requests inside one department.Details
Problem

One department needed a cleaner way to organise HR-related requests before they reached the central HR area.

Change

Helped structure a support sub-sector with defined points of contact for HR intake and follow-up.

Result

Requests became easier to classify, route and resolve because staff knew where to start and who was responsible.

Transfer

This maps directly to manager support, employee lifecycle administration and case routing in a multidisciplinary organisation.

07Confidential Enquiries and De-escalationHandling upset employees without making the record messy.Details
  1. 01

    Listen without rushing the answer

    Employees often arrive frustrated because HR is the entry point for a practical problem. The first job is to understand the issue clearly.

  2. 02

    Separate feeling from process

    Stay calm, identify the actual record, deadline, document or decision affected, and avoid giving unsupported answers.

  3. 03

    Solve or route

    Resolve what is within scope; route sensitive, conduct, performance or legal matters to the appropriate person.

  4. 04

    Keep the channel formal

    Use proper communication channels so the matter is traceable and private details are not spread informally.

08Banked Hours ControlA small spreadsheet fix that made follow-up cleaner.Details
Before

Banked hours and related follow-up could become hard to compare when information moved through different routines.

Change

Standardised spreadsheet controls and reorganised the supporting system flow so information was easier to check.

Result

Less back-and-forth, clearer exceptions and lower risk of avoidable overtime or correction work.

Lesson

Small controls matter when they protect payroll, attendance and manager decisions from messy inputs.

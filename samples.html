01HR Advice Response NoteA simple structure for answering routine employee or manager enquiries.Details
Purpose

Give practical guidance without guessing, overpromising or losing the record trail.

Question

What is being asked, by whom, and what decision or deadline is affected?

Source

Which policy, procedure, Award, agreement, delegation or manager instruction applies?

Answer

Plain-language guidance, required documents, next step, owner and expected timeframe.

Record

Where the response is saved, what remains open and when follow-up is due.

"Based on the current procedure, the next step is to confirm the required document and effective date. Once that is complete, the payroll-relevant information can be prepared for the next processing deadline."

05Employee Records QA ChecklistA practical quality check for HR records before reporting or payroll handoff.Details
Completeness

Is the record whole?

Required documents, dates, approvals and employee details are present or clearly flagged.

Consistency

Do fields agree?

Role, department, status, dates and movement type do not contradict each other.

Traceability

Can it be checked later?

The source, owner, date, action and follow-up status are easy to find.

06Policy-to-Action Interpretation MemoA careful way to convert rules into operational steps.Details
Rule source

Policy, Award, agreement, legislation, delegation or internal procedure.

Operational question

What must HR, Payroll, the manager or the employee actually do?

Decision points

Eligibility, dates, approvals, evidence, exceptions and escalation threshold.

Record outcome

Which system, document, note or report should show that the action was completed?

Australian context: I would treat Awards, Enterprise Agreements and local policies as primary sources to be read carefully, checked with the right internal owner and translated into practical steps.

07Confidential Case Intake and TriageHow to keep sensitive matters structured without overstepping authority.Details
  1. 01

    Capture the request

    Record who raised it, broad category, urgency and immediate risk, without unnecessary detail.

  2. 02

    Limit visibility

    Keep access to those with a genuine need to know and avoid informal sharing.

  3. 03

    Route correctly

    Escalate to the right HR lead, manager or specialist when the matter involves ER, performance, conduct or investigation.

  4. 04

    Track next action

    Keep dates, owner and status visible while respecting confidentiality.

Local Government HR Operations

A practical view of how I structure personnel routines for a large public-sector workforce: intake, records, payroll-relevant inputs, reporting, follow-up and confidentiality.

Prepared by Murilo Modena Boscato Public-sector people operations · 1,500+ employees · seven departments
Privacy note

This case is generalised and privacy-safe. It does not include employee names, identifiers, private cases, internal documents or system screenshots.

01 · Scope

People operations across a complex public organisation.

My current role covers personnel routines for more than 1,500 employees across seven municipal departments. The work depends on accuracy, timing, discretion and clear communication.

Workforce1,500+

Personnel routines supported at municipal scale.

Departments7

Administration, finance, social services, infrastructure, planning, culture and other public operations.

Core workRecords

Employee files, attendance, leave information, payroll inputs and reports.

EnvironmentRegulated

Processes tied to legislation, internal procedure, deadlines and formal documentation.

02 · Operating rhythm

How a request becomes a closed record.

  1. 01

    Receive and classify

    Identify whether the request relates to records, leave, attendance, onboarding, payroll input, manager support or a confidential matter.

  2. 02

    Check the source

    Review the applicable policy, legislation, procedure or internal rule before giving a practical answer.

  3. 03

    Validate documents

    Confirm that required forms, approvals and supporting information are complete enough to move forward.

  4. 04

    Update or prepare input

    Record the change, prepare payroll-relevant information or route the item to the correct owner.

  5. 05

    Communicate and close

    Give a clear answer, record the follow-up and keep pending items visible until they are resolved.

Clean handoff prevents downstream errors.

The exact fields change by system. The discipline does not: confirm authority, record the source, check dates and keep payroll-relevant information complete.

05 · Confidentiality

Public examples must be safer than internal work.

Remove

Direct identifiers

Names, IDs, signatures, addresses, contact details, personal numbers and recognisable document references stay out of public material.

Generalise

Quasi-identifiers

Rare roles, exact dates, small teams, location details and salary information are widened or converted into categories.

Separate

Method from data

The portfolio shows workflows, checklists and reporting logic, not live employee data or private case notes.

06 · First 30 days

First month in the contract.

Days 1-7

Learn the local rules

  • Review policies, delegations, Awards and agreements
  • Understand HRIS fields and reporting definitions
  • Map key contacts across HR, Payroll and Talent Acquisition
Days 8-20

Stabilise the rhythm

  • Track open requests, employee movements and deadlines
  • Check record quality and common data gaps
  • Support managers with clear written follow-up
Days 21-30

Improve what is visible

  • Propose reporting or checklist improvements
  • Clarify recurring handoff points with Payroll
  • Document repeat questions into practical guidance

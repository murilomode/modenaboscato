Luxury F&B Outlet
Operations Playbook

A practical framework for consistent service, confident teams and commercially aware guest experiences across a luxury lobby bar or afternoon tea outlet.

Prepared by Murilo Modena Boscato Hospitality operations application sample
Scope note

This is an independent demonstration of operating judgement. It uses no proprietary hotel information, internal targets or claimed performance results.

01 · Service intent

Make refinement feel effortless.

Luxury is built through anticipation, consistency and recovery, not ceremony alone.

01

See the guest

Read context, preferences and pace before offering assistance.

02

Own the moment

Take responsibility for requests and keep the guest informed.

03

Coordinate quietly

Use clear internal communication without exposing operational friction.

04

Close the loop

Confirm satisfaction, record insight and brief the next shift.

02 · Daily rhythm

A shift with clear control points.

  1. Pre-open

    Readiness walk

    Presentation, reservations, stock, equipment, safety, team allocation and known guest preferences.

  2. Briefing

    Align the floor

    Service focus, VIPs, menu availability, commercial opportunity, role ownership and recovery authority.

  3. Service

    Observe and rebalance

    Track pace, guest sentiment, section load, wait times and opportunities to support or coach.

  4. Close

    Reconcile and learn

    Cash and POS checks, stock variance, incidents, maintenance, guest feedback and next-shift handover.

03 · Team briefing

Ten focused minutes before service.

Guests

Reservations, celebrations, accessibility needs, preferences and priority arrivals.

Product

Unavailable items, features, allergens, pairings and one natural upsell focus.

People

Sections, responsibilities, breaks, support signals and coaching focus.

Standards

One service behaviour to protect, demonstrated in practical language.

Risk

RSA, food safety, incidents, equipment issues and escalation points.

Energy

Recognise a contribution and finish with one clear shared priority.

04 · Service recovery

Recover trust, then learn.

  1. Listen

    Give full attention and clarify the impact without defensiveness.

  2. Own

    Acknowledge the experience and take responsibility for the next action.

  3. Resolve

    Offer an appropriate remedy within authority or escalate with context.

  4. Confirm

    Return to the guest and verify that the recovery has worked.

  5. Record

    Capture the cause, action and learning for the team and next shift.

06 · First 90 days

Listen first. Improve with the team.

Days 1-30

Observe and baseline

  • Learn standards, systems and service rituals
  • Meet the team and partner departments
  • Map guest feedback and operational friction
  • Understand budget, roster and compliance controls
Days 31-60

Coach and refine

  • Strengthen briefing and handover rhythm
  • Clarify recovery authority and escalation
  • Test one service or commercial improvement
  • Recognise consistent colleague behaviours
Days 61-90

Sustain and improve

  • Review scorecard trends with the team
  • Standardise proven improvements
  • Build development priorities by colleague
  • Present the next quarter's operating focus
